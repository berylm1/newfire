DRAFT BRIEF — MOTION TO DISMISS

The principle of judicial review, as established in Marbury v. Madison, gives
this Court the authority to determine the constitutionality of legislative
acts. Plaintiff's claim fails to state a cognizable injury under the standard
articulated in Lujan v. Defenders of Wildlife.

Furthermore, this matter is squarely controlled by Smithson v. General
Fabricated Holdings Corp., which held that a purely speculative future harm
cannot support standing in a case of this type.

For these reasons, the motion to dismiss should be granted.
